The Hand Behind
The Harvest.

In a world addicted to the synthetic, I chose the living.

My name is Bajak. I do not run a factory; I tend a garden. The industry told me that high-performance sports gear required plastic, petrochemicals, and mass manufacturing. I refused to accept that the tools we use to master our bodies should be toxic to our world.

I created the BAJAK Atelier in my home because true innovation requires silence. Here, I don't just assemble cases—I grow them. Every imperfection in the grain is a record of the organism's life.

— Bajak, Mester.

From Spore to Stitch

The 45-Day Cycle of Creation.

01
Inoculation

Selected agricultural substrate is seeded with mycelium spores in the dark of the studio.

12
The Bind

The mycelium web consumes the substrate, binding it into a solid biological foam.

25
The Cure

Harvested, compressed, and tanned using natural oils to create a water-resistant skin.

40
The Stitch

I cut the pattern and stitch it with waxed linen thread. No machines. Just tension and time.
